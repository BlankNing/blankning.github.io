I am a PhD student at the University of Bristol, supervised by [Dr Edmund R. Hunt](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=H9IaseQAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao) and [Dr Nikolai WF Bode](https://scholar.google.co.uk/citations?user=JdkAYUoAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao). My research sits at the intersection of multi-robot planning and human-robot interaction.

## Research Interests

My work spans two interconnected areas:

**Multi-robot planning** — I am interested in problems such as multi-agent path finding (MAPF) and multi-robot patrol, where a team of robots must coordinate to achieve shared objectives efficiently and safely.

**Human-robot interaction** — As robots increasingly integrate into human environments, understanding how to design robot systems that respond appropriately to human behaviour becomes essential. Current model-free, black-box approaches to this problem have significant limitations: they lack interpretability and generalisability, making it difficult to reason about safety and trust.

## Research Vision

My goal is to bridge the gap between purely autonomous multi-robot systems and the messy, social reality of shared human spaces. I pursue this through two complementary approaches:

- **Behavioural experiments and agent-based modelling** to unravel the underlying principles of human behaviour in robot-populated environments.
- **Incorporating autonomy, cooperation, and negotiation** into existing multi-robot tasks, so that robotic automation systems can meaningfully interact with human beings.

I believe that grounding robot system design in empirically validated models of human behaviour — rather than learned black-box policies — is key to building robots that are safe, transparent, and socially acceptable.
